# global-inequality-scientific-poster (simulation-based study)

# Does Place of Birth Matter? – Education, Knowledge and Well-being
Project Overview: Visual Analysis of Global Inequalities

### Author: Monika Griecs-Farkas

Location: Winchester, United Kingdom

## Objective This project examines how birth location fundamentally shapes life chances, analysing the correlations between literacy, education levels, and the happiness index across different regions.

## Methodology & Format The project is presented as a scientific poster created using Tableau and PowerPoint. To ensure deep analytical value, the visual data is supported by an integrated 1,000-word mini-study.

Key Findings * Literacy Gaps: Data highlights that in regions such as the Central African Republic, only 38.5% of young people are literate.

Gender Disparity: Significant gaps exist between male and female literacy (e.g., 29% for women vs 48% for men in certain areas).

Educational Quality: In countries like Niger, quality is critically low, with only 9% of students meeting reading and 27% meeting mathematics thresholds.

Happiness vs. Education: While education is linked to well-being, knowledge alone does not guarantee happiness. In areas facing conflict or extreme instability, the positive impact of learning is nullified by the struggle for daily survival.

## Conclusion Place of birth is a decisive factor in a person's future. The analysis proves that without social stability and security, education alone cannot deliver global well-being.
